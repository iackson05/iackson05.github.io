Introduction

Hello, my name is Jackson Smith, I am a Computer Science student at the University of Guelph and this is my report regarding the first four months of my IT Technician co-op placement at the Toronto Pan Am Sports Centre (which I will refer to as TPASC throughout this report) in the Summer of 2025. TPASC is a sports facility based out of Scarborough, ON and is the home of the Scarborough Shooting Stars of the CEBL and hosts many large events such as the World Aquatics Swimming World Cup.

My role at TPASC provided me with the chance to put many of the skills that I have learned as a student to use, varying from technical skills like script writing or computer maintnance to soft skills like time management and leadership.

About TPASC

Company Overview

The Toronto Pan Am Sports Centre was built in 2014 to host the Pan Am Games, and has since been used as a public athletics centre that that sees over 300,000 customers a year. The facility is owned by the City of Toronto and the University of Toronto.

About the facility

The facility consists of the Field House capable of hosting all of the games for the Scarborough Shooting Stars, drop-in sports, and other large events such as the orientation for the students attending UofT Scarborough. There is also an Aquatic Centre that has a training pool, competition pool, and a dive tank. The competition pool and the dive tank see many large events each year, such as the World Aquatics Swimming World Cup that will occur in October 2025. Lastly, there is a fitness centre that has countless members visit daily.

There are roughly 70 Full-Time and 300 Part-Time employees keeping the facility running. I have met so many great people during my time working at the facility and the staff was very welcoming to the arrival of myself and the other handful of interns that TPASC took on in the summer.

Learning Goals & Outcomes

At the beginning of my work term, I established three key learning goals to guide my professional development:

Goal 1: Networking

Objective: I wanted to develop a robust understanding of networking concepts.

Approach: I approached this goal by getting to know how the networking infrastructure works at the facility (by both physically discovering the components and by familiarizing myself with our Cisco Meraki portal and our LibreNMS server) and trying to test the knowledge that I gained through a project.

Outcome: I still have a ways to go in terms of reaching this goal, but I was able to put my knowledge to the test by creating a network dashboard for my team on our LibreNMS server. This dashboard consists of a comprehensive network map, device port status updates, server utilizations, and a system log. This project taught me how network components work together (with the network map), how devices are able to connect to our network, how to ping devices for their status using SNMP, and some basic networking concepts (like VLANs, subnets, routing, etc.).

Goal 2: Leadership

Objective: I wanted to better my leadership skills

Approach: I approached this goal by trying my best to take initiative and to approach my work with a strong attitude.

Outcome: To me, leadership isn't completely about leading a group people, but also their work ethic and attitude. I made sure that in every task on my plate, I approached everything with a high level of intensity and made sure that my work was completed to the highest degree. I believe that the best leaders are not just able to lead others, but also themselves. By approaching my work in such a fashion, the skills that I gained will help me when I am in a position to lead others. I need to be able to lead myself before I can be trusted to lead another individual.

Goal 3: Personal Organization/Time Management

Objective: I wanted to develop stronger time management skills and be a more organized person.

Approach: I made sure to layout and detail each of my tasks and create a timeline/checklist to keep track of my progress and make sure that I was on track to finishing my tasks on time.

Outcome: I think I developed really strong time organization skills by following this process. I got all of my work done with ample time to spare, which gave me the opportunity to take on more work than expected by manager. I think this skill will immediately help me become a better student when it comes to managing my time and workload and I should be a more productive and efficient student because of this experience.

About the Role

Responsibilities

My role at TPASC was titled as IT Technician. The main purpose of my role was to create helpful solutions for the rest of the staff. I have created multiple solutions/workflows using Microsoft's Power Apps and SharePoint Online. I am also available for IT support when requested. I gained experience in setting up and maintaining AV systems. The role is very broad and always keeps me on my toes, but I am very grateful that I am able to assist the large staff pool of TPASC to hopefully make some lives easier.

As an IT Technician, each day always brings new tasks and problems to solve. My typical day will consist of working on some project, but other things will come up during the day that require my assistance (i.e. computer help/troubleshooting, being an extra set of hands in another project, etc.). My day at work tends to fly by as I don't feel like I have much downtime. But I love every second of it and love being able to make an impact on others.

Key Projects

The first project I was assigned at TPASC was to create a calendar in SharePoint Online to hold all of the major events occurring at the facility and meeting room bookings. This project allowed me to showcase some of the programming skills that I have developed as a student to write a script that polls our facility management software, EZFacility, to get event info, and post it to the SharePoint Calendar. I hosted this calendar on AWS to automate the process and make it so that all info on the calendar is up to date. Network Dashboard, Wayfinding Kiosks, Global Calendar, Automated Workflows/digitization of forms

The second project that I worked on is still ongoing and unfinished, but there are plans to have it in place and up and running before the end of my tenure. It is an automated workflow to handle assignments of onboarding and offboarding tasks for staff. This project taught me how to utilize Power Automate and create useful automations.

Another project that I worked on was to create a network dashboard. This project was assigned to me so that I could learn the foundation of a network and how it functions. Here I learned things like how my web search requests get routed and how I am able to access the internet, what pieces are needed to create a network, and how to poll devices to get their status using SNMP. I discovered the entirety of our network, including the 150+ access points, 15 servers, 2 firewalls and 15+ switches and how all of these technologies can communicate amongst each other.

Some other projects that I have been involved in include setting up wayfinding kiosks for the facility and tracking the IT assets within the facility to create a dashboard in Power BI to easily show replacement forecasts and device statuses, digitizing existing forms using SPFX and JS and using Power Automate to create workflows based on specific criteria in the submission, and I serviced and tagged over 50 of our assets (desktops, laptops, etc.).

Interesting Aspects

Some of the most interesting aspects of the job include:

• Technology and Tools: Including LibreNMS, Cisco Meraki, SPFX, Office 365, VMware/HyperV, etc.

• Team Collaboration: I spent quite some time working among other staff members in other departments such as managing our TVs and the content they play with the Marketing Team, and working with the Administration Team to create a useful event calendar.

• Learning Opportunities: I have developed lots of Networking and Cybersecurity experience, as well as how to diagnose computers and assist others with their technical needs.

• Atmosphere: Working in a sports facility has been really cool as I get to make an impact in some of the major events that we host and it's cool knowing that some of the work I am doing is affecting/helping lots of people.

Conclusion

I have had an eye-opening semester working at TPASC, it has been really cool working in an office and learning the business of a sports facility of their amplitude. I have learned so much on the job about the field of Computer Science and I am excited to continue learning during my second work term here. I think the skills I have both developed and learned will help me in my career in Computer Science.
